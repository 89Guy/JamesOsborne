About

James Osborne

I have extensive experience in the field of criminal law and criminal procedure. I spent over thirty years in the U.S. Navy retiring at the rank of Captain. As an intelligence officer, I spent almost 10 years as a federal agent for the Naval Criminal Investigative Service (NCIS) conducting criminal, counterintelligence, and counter-terrorism investigations. I was also a police officer in the City of Stockton for 12 years and retired as a Sergeant.
I taught criminal law and criminal procedure to high school and college students, enhancing my knowledge and application of those subjects.

I focus my legal practice on criminal defense, DUI, and personal injury, but I also practice family law and unlawful detainers. I am well-versed in litigation, and I am a skilled negotiator.

I have a Bachelor of Public Administration degree with a major in Criminal Justice from the University of Mississippi, a Master of Arts in National Security Affairs from the Naval Postgraduate School in Monterey and a Juris Doctorate from Humphreys University, Laurence Drivon School of Law. I am a life member of the Delta Theta Phi legal fraternity, the Peace Officers Research Association of California (PORAC), and a member of the Bar Associations in San Joaquin, Alameda, and Stanislaus counties.
I am licensed to practice in the State of California and in Federal District Court in the Eastern and Northern Districts of California.

CRIMINAL LAW

The criminal justice system in the United States is probably the finest in the world. The Constitution of the United States provides for many protections for those accused of crimes. My job is to ensure your rights are protected and the procedures for arrest, charging and trial are applied equally to every defendant.

Without legal counsel a person charged with a crime may make mistakes which could cost their freedom, property, and rights. I will give you advice, counsel, and representation to defend you from criminal charges.

PERSONAL INJURY

Mistakes and accidents happen, sometimes through carelessness or negligence. The person responsible for your injury and his or her insurance company are not your friend. They are going to try to limit their damages and payments for your injuries. My job is to make sure you are fairly compensated for your injuries.

Do not accept a settlement from the insurance company until you have consulted with an attorney.

UNLAWFUL DETAINER

Property owners who rent or lease their real property to others always face challenges from renters who do not pay the rent, damage the property or are just a nuisance to the property owner and the community. The law protects both renters and landlords, but tenants generally get the priority.

Since the pandemic it has been very difficult for landlords to evict tenants for non-payment of rent, or for most other reasons. I know how to lawfully evict tenants for a variety of reasons and can help the property owner recover their property and damages.

Labour law

Divorce is difficult for both parties, but because California is a no-fault divorce state, either can file and will be granted a divorce. California is also a community property state so division of property can be difficult.

Issues of spousal support, child custody and support, and division of property cause what could be simple procedure into a very difficult situation that needs someone with experience and skills in negotiating to achieve the best results for all parties, including the children.
